There is a statistical difference between control and treatment for all morphological trait

  • SL: Shoot length is significantly reduced in the salt-stressed plants compared with the control (p < 0.05). The mean value for SL in salt-stressed plants is 1.10 ± and 2.30 ± for control plants. SL stress index of accessions varied from 16.73 to 89.12

  • RL: Root length is significantly reduced in the salt-stressed plants compared with the control (p < 0.05). The mean value for RL in salt-stressed plants is 0.98 ± and 2.17 ± for control plants. RL stress index of accessions varied from 15.91 to 73.53

  • PH: Plant height is significantly reduced in the salt-stressed plants compared with the control (p < 0.05). The mean value for PH in salt-stressed plants is 2.18 ± and 4.43 ± for control plants. PH stress index of accessions varied from 19.83 to 74.07

  • NL: Number of leaves is significantly reduced in the salt-stressed plants compared with the control (p < 0.05). The mean value for NL in salt-stressed plants is 2.43 ± and 4.22 ± for control plants. NL stress index of accessions varied from 28.00 to 94.12

  • LA: Leaf area is significantly reduced in the salt-stressed plants compared with the control (p < 0.05). The mean value for LA in salt-stressed plants is 0.03 ± and 0.07 ± for control plants. LA stress index of accessions varied from 75.95 to 100
